Working Papers

“Information, Managerial Incentives, and Scale: Evidence from Hotel Pricing” [Dropbox]

  • New identification results to infer firms' beliefs over demand from prices and quantities-- even if the mapping from beliefs to prices is stochastic and firm-specific.
  • Some firms are better at predicting demand than others. In the hotel industry, informational differences are large.
  • All else equal, bigger firms (hotel chains) know more. But this is masked by weaker incentives for managers in large chains to gather information.

“Volatility, Uncertainty, and Hotel Capacity Choice” [Dropbox]

  • Capacity is sunk-- so when demand changes, it cannot change. So how does demand stochasticity distort capacity choice?
  • In the hotel industry, it doesn't, so long as prices can adjust instead. But since prices cannot adjust to demand shifts, hotels build excess capacity in anticipation of stochastic demand.
  • Theory shows that this is because excess capacity is cheap and often slack. In other markets, this may not be true.
